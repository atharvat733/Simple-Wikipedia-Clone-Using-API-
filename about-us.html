About Us

Wikipedia is a free online encyclopedia, created and edited by volunteers around the world and hosted by the Wikimedia Foundation. Our mission is to provide free, reliable, and neutral information to everyone, everywhere. Wikipedia articles are written collaboratively by volunteers and cover a wide range of topics, including history, science, arts, technology, and much more.

Since its launch in 2001, Wikipedia has grown to become one of the largest and most popular reference websites on the internet, with millions of articles in multiple languages. We believe in the power of knowledge-sharing and the importance of open access to information for the betterment of society.

Wikipedia operates on a principle of neutrality, aiming to present information from a neutral point of view and to provide citations or references for all information presented. This ensures that readers can verify the information provided and make informed judgments.

In addition to its main encyclopedia, Wikipedia also hosts other projects, including Wikimedia Commons (a repository of free media files) andWikidata (a structured database). These projects further contribute to the Wikimedia Foundation's mission of making knowledge freely available to everyone.
